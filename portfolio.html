Yellow Solitude

It was past noon and the light was wrong by every standard rule — harsh, flat, unforgiving. But this bloom had turned away from everything, facing a direction no one expected. I waited twenty minutes for a cloud that never came. Then I pressed anyway. Sometimes the wrong light finds the right moment.
